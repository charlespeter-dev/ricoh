Automating High-Volume Document Processing in the Cloud

DHL Supply Chain Iberia processes thousands of delivery notes annually across 53 warehouses throughout Spain. Their manual data entry approach was labour-intensive, error-prone, and struggled to keep pace with the expanding e-commerce demands.

In this case study, learn how DHL partnered with Ricoh to deploy DocuWare Cloud—transforming their delivery note management from a time-consuming bottleneck into an automated, scalable system that reduced processing time drastically whilst virtually eliminating errors.

DocuWare Cloud's features were a perfect fit for our operational needs. Its ease of use and speed of implementation provide significant added value.
Juan Manuel Sen, IT Project Delivery Manager, DHL Supply Chain Iberia

An Overview:

DHL Supply Chain Iberia, a subsidiary of Deutsche Post DHL Group, operates 53 warehouses with over 3,500 employees across Spain. As a logistics leader in logistics, they serve customers from all sectors with tailored solutions—and rely on innovative technology partners to support the growing demands of e-commerce operations.

The Challenges

Managing thousands of delivery notes manually

Processing delivery notes is critical for order verification, traceability, and warehouse management. However, DHL's manual approach required two full-time employees working eight hours daily to handle data entry, with three of their largest customers alone responsible for 84,000 documents annually—some of these notes exceeding 30 pages. These documents arrived through multiple channels, slowing down processes and increasing error rates, while rapid e-commerce growth meant they urgently needed a scalable, cloud-based solution.

The Solution

Using DocuWare Cloud to accelerate processes

DHL selected DocuWare Cloud, implemented by Ricoh, to eliminate manual data entry bottlenecks through automated capture and intelligent indexing. It integrated seamlessly with DHL's existing ERP system, enabling robotic process automation (RPA) for data extraction and verification. This had the added benefit of standardising warehouse operations while providing automatic alerts and notifications.

The Outcomes

Drastic error-reduction with minimal corrections

DHL’s delivery note processing was transformed almost immediately. Manual work was reduced from two full-time employees doing nothing but processing all day to just one person working half a day on the same task. Employees could now focus on value-added work, even as automated workflows dramatically improved data quality and processing speed.

DocuWare features and benefits

Efficient document processing: Reduced manual processing time by 75%, freeing staff for higher-value activities.

Improved accuracy and quality: Eliminated manual data entry errors and enabled faster, more reliable warehouse processes.

Enhanced security and control: Automated governance with role assignments, alerts, and notifications for complete traceability.

Scalable and flexible: Standardised processes across multiple sites with a solution that grows with business needs.

Conclusion

Future-ready document management at scale

DocuWare Cloud has empowered DHL Supply Chain Iberia's digital transformation with streamlined, error-free document processing that scales with them. Its success has led to deployment across multiple DHL customers in Spain and recognition as a best practice to be presented at the European level.

It is a user-centric solution that covers very specific needs, which are met with very advanced, high-quality technology. Ricoh has been collaborating with DHL in another more traditional line of services for some time, in a relationship that has always been very positive and enriching, so we have not hesitated to move forward with more cutting-edge services.
Juan Manuel Sen, IT Project Delivery Manager, DHL Supply Chain Iberia
